Hiring the right electrician for your home or business can save you time, money, and frustration. This guide covers what to look for, the right questions to ask, and red flags to avoid when selecting an electrical contractor in West Michigan.

## Verify Licensing and Insurance

Always confirm your electrician is licensed and insured before work begins. A Michigan electrical license confirms they've met state training and testing requirements. Insurance protects you if something goes wrong during the job.

You can verify Michigan electrical contractor licenses through the state licensing board. Our MI License is #6220430.

## Ask for a Clear Scope Before Work Begins

A professional electrician should be able to describe exactly what they're going to do before starting. This includes materials, circuit work, permits needed, and how long the job will take. Vague estimates are a warning sign.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

- Are you licensed and insured in Michigan?
- Will you pull permits for this work?
- Can you describe the full scope in writing?
- Have you done similar projects before?
- What happens if unexpected issues arise mid-project?

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Requesting full payment upfront before any work is completed
- Unable or unwilling to explain the scope clearly
- No license or insurance documentation available
- Pressure to start immediately without a written estimate
- No references or verifiable reviews

## The Value of Owner-Operated vs. Large Crews

Owner-operated electrical companies often provide clearer communication and more consistent quality because the person you speak to is the same person doing the work. This can reduce miscommunication, change orders, and scope creep.

## What to Expect from a Good Electrician

- Clear, polite communication throughout the job
- Permit coordination and inspection handling
- Clean worksite habits (no mess left behind)
- A walkthrough before and after the work
- Follow-up availability if questions arise
